        Reaxys Search Endeavours

        Published by Rachel Brennesholtz on 02 December 2024 16:42 3 min read

        Written By Maziar Moradi Fard, Senior Data Scientist; Christian Druckenbrodt, Manager Data Quality Standards and Informatics

        c.druckenbrodt@elsevier.com 

         

        Revolutionizing Reaxys Search: A Leap into Semantic Technology 

        In the ever-evolving landscape of artificial intelligence, our commitment to enhancing product performance has achieved a new milestone. Historically, search functionality has been a cornerstone of our business, yet we had not yet fully harnessed the potential of advanced AI technologies—until now. Our latest initiative focuses on transforming the Reaxys search experience by implementing cutting-edge semantic search technology, aimed at delivering users more relevant and accurate results. 

         

        Traditionally, our search relied on a lexical approach, which matched words in user queries to those in our database. While straightforward, this method often faltered when it faced misspellings or variations in word order, leading to user frustration. In contrast, semantic search delves deeper, capturing the intent behind user queries and aligning them with the content of our documents. This shift allows us to accommodate typos and variations, ultimately creating a more intuitive and user-friendly experience. 

         

        Designing a Robust Search Framework 

        At the core of our semantic search strategy is vector search, powered by advanced deep learning techniques. We faced a pivotal decision: should we utilize a pretrained model as-is, or fine-tune it on our specialized Reaxys dataset? Initial experiments showed the superior outcomes of fine-tuning, we opted for the latter. However, gathering the extensive training data required posed a significant challenge. 

         

        To overcome this hurdle, we leveraged state-of-the-art large language models (LLMs) to generate a training set of domain-specific queries that simulate user behaviour. This innovative approach allowed us to create a robust dataset for fine-tuning, ensuring that our model could accurately extract relevant documents based on user inquiries. Furthermore, we integrated additional microservices into our search stack to re-rank and refine results, guaranteeing that users receive the most pertinent information. 

         

        Our efforts culminated in the successful vectorization of 15 million Reaxys data entries, which are now linked to a user interface for internal evaluation and feedback. As we approach the Beta release of this enhanced search functionality, we are excited to expand our capabilities to encompass the entire Reaxys dataset in our new search pipeline. 

        Streamlining Evaluation with LLMs 

        In the fast-paced world of development, thorough evaluation is paramount. Traditional qualitative assessment by Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) is needed but can be cumbersome, often requiring numerous experts and raising concerns about query diversity and Inter-Rater Reliability (IRR). To streamline this process, our Data Sciences Life Sciences (DSLS) team has introduced a groundbreaking "Evaluation by LLM Jury" framework. 

         

        This innovative framework automates up to 80% of the evaluation workload, allowing SMEs to concentrate on resolving discrepancies where LLMs may disagree. Acting as evaluators, LLMs swiftly analyzed a diverse test set with an array of queries across 15 chemistry domains, from organic to medicinal to polymer chemistry. Each query is meticulously crafted in variations of length and complexity, including also typographical errors. 

         

        The results of our domain-tuned vector database approach have been promising, effectively addressing complex natural language queries in the chemistry domain whilst being able to deal well with typos. The full story was shared recently by Mark Sheehan at the Pistoia Alliance conference in Philadelphia in November. 

         

         

        Conclusion: A New Era for Reaxys 

        In summary, our innovative approach to domain-tuned search technology significantly improving performance compared to non-domain-tuned vector strategies. Hereby, enhancing user experience and positions us to fully leverage AI's potential in our products.  

        The “Evaluation by LLM Jury” framework represents a substantial leap forward in our evaluation capabilities, harmonizing the precision of AI with the nuanced understanding of human experts.

        This innovation places us at the forefront of efficient and effective evaluation, ultimately enhancing our service delivery and customer satisfaction. 

         

        As we continue to refine our search capabilities, we are excited about the future of Reaxys and the transformative impact our advancements will have on our users. Together, we are not just improving search functionality; we are redefining the way our customers interact with information in the realm of chemistry.
